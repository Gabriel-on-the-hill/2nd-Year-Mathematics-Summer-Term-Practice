2022 · Q510 min Balloon pump — cylinder & sphere

A balloon pump is a cylinder with internal diameter 6 cm and height 20 cm. Each pump passes one full cylinder of air into a balloon.

(a) Show that the volume of one full cylinder of air is 180π cm³.

(b)(i) Darragh inflates a spherical balloon of radius 15 cm. Find its volume in cm³, in terms of π.

(b)(ii) Darragh pumps once per second. How many seconds to fully inflate?

(c) Gustav's bigger spherical balloon is fully inflated after 50 seconds. Find Gustav's balloon radius, to 1 d.p.

Worked solution

(a) Radius = 3 cm (half of 6). $V = \pi r^2 h = \pi(3)^2(20) = 180\pi$ cm³. ✓

(b)(i) $V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 = \frac{4}{3}\pi(15)^3 = \frac{4}{3}\pi(3375) = 4500\pi$ cm³.

(b)(ii) Pumps needed $= \dfrac{4500\pi}{180\pi} = 25$. 25 seconds

(c) After 50 pumps: volume $= 50 \times 180\pi = 9000\pi$ cm³. So $\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 = 9000\pi$ → $r^3 = 6750$ → $r = \sqrt[3]{6750}$. r ≈ 18.9 cm

(b) The wax is recast into cylindrical candles of radius $r$ cm and height 9 cm. Find the volume of each candle in terms of $r$ and $\pi$.

(c) 100 candles are made; 10% of the wax is wasted. Find $r$, to 1 d.p.

Worked solution

2024 · Q710 min Cube & cuboid — volume, surface area, hidden cuboid

Exam Style8 min Cone — volume, slant height & surface area

A cone-shaped sports pylon has base radius 6 cm and perpendicular height 8 cm.

(a) Find its volume, in terms of $\pi$ and correct to 1 d.p.

(b) Find the slant height $\ell$.

(c) Find the total surface area of the cone, to 1 d.p.

2022 · Q125 min Triangle in a circle (diameter, Pythagoras, trig)

Circle $k$ has area $25\pi$ cm². $A, B, C$ lie on $k$, with $[AB]$ a diameter and $|AC| = 8$ cm. Find the smallest angle in triangle $ABC$.

Worked solution

Area = $\pi r^2 = 25\pi$ → $r = 5$ → diameter $|AB| = 10$ cm.

By the theorem "angle in a semicircle = 90°", $\angle ACB = 90°$. So triangle $ABC$ is right-angled at $C$ with hypotenuse $|AB| = 10$, leg $|AC| = 8$.

Pythagoras: $|BC|^2 = 10^2 - 8^2 = 36$ → $|BC| = 6$ cm.

Smallest angle is opposite the smallest side. Smallest side is $|BC| = 6$, opposite vertex $A$.
$\sin A = \dfrac{6}{10} = 0.6$ → A ≈ 36.87°

2023 · Q710 min Proving ABCD is a square; converse

$A, B, C, D$ are four points. $A$ and $B$ shown on the grid (with $A=(1,1)$, $B=(6,-1)$); $C = (8, 4)$, $D = (3, 6)$.

(a)(i) Plot $C(8, 4)$ and $D(3, 6)$. (ii) Write coords of $A$ and $B$.

(b) Show that $ABCD$ is a square by:

  • (i) showing $|AB| = |BC|$;
  • (ii) showing $AB \perp BC$ (without measuring).

(c) Ciarán says "Every parallelogram is a square."

  • (i) True or false? Justify.
  • (ii) Write the converse.

Worked solution

(a)(ii) A = (1, 1), B = (6, −1)

(b)(i) $|AB| = \sqrt{(6-1)^2 + (-1-1)^2} = \sqrt{25 + 4} = \sqrt{29}$.
$|BC| = \sqrt{(8-6)^2 + (4-(-1))^2} = \sqrt{4 + 25} = \sqrt{29}$. So $|AB| = |BC|$. ✓

(b)(ii) Slope $AB = \dfrac{-1-1}{6-1} = -\dfrac{2}{5}$. Slope $BC = \dfrac{4-(-1)}{8-6} = \dfrac{5}{2}$.
Product = $-\dfrac{2}{5} \cdot \dfrac{5}{2} = -1$. Slopes multiply to $-1$, so $AB \perp BC$. ✓

(c)(i) False. A parallelogram only requires opposite sides parallel — a rectangle (non-square) is a parallelogram that isn't a square.

(c)(ii) Converse: "Every square is a parallelogram." (This statement is actually true.)

2023 · Q1210 min Reuleaux triangle — symmetry construction & arc length

(a) Using only compass and straight edge, construct one axis of symmetry. Show all construction lines.

(b)(i) Write down $\angle ACB$.  (b)(ii) $|BC| = 40$ cm. Find the length of the circular arc from $B$ to $C$, to 2 d.p.

Worked solution

(a) Pick any vertex (say $A$). Construct the perpendicular bisector of the opposite side $[BC]$ (centre arcs from $B$ and $C$ with equal radius, draw line through their intersection points). This line is an axis of symmetry — it passes through $A$ and the midpoint of $BC$, splitting the shape into two mirror halves.

(b)(i) Triangle $ABC$ is equilateral → ∠ACB = 60°

(b)(ii) Arc from $B$ to $C$ has centre $A$, radius $|AB| = 40$ cm, subtending the 60° angle at $A$.
Arc length $= \dfrac{60}{360} \cdot 2\pi(40) = \dfrac{80\pi}{6} = \dfrac{40\pi}{3}$.
Arc length ≈ 41.89 cm

2025 · Q610 min Construction C7; angles in parallel lines; similar triangles

(a) Divide segment $[PQ]$ into three equal lengths, without measuring. Show all construction lines. (Construction 7 — HL only.)

In triangle $ABC$, $D, E$ lie on $[AB]$ and $F, G$ lie on $[AC]$, with $DF \parallel EG \parallel BC$. $\angle DAF = 28°$, $\angle ABC = 72°$.

(b)(i) Find $x$ (at $D$), $y$ (at $F$), $z$ (at $E$).

(b)(ii) Explain why triangles $ABC$ and $ADF$ are similar but not congruent.

(c) Given $|AD| = |DE| = |EB| = 10$ and $|BC| = 14.3$. Using similar triangles, find $|GE|$.

Worked solution

(a) Draw any ray from $P$ at a small angle to $PQ$. With compass set to any fixed radius, step off 3 equal arcs along the ray — call the points $P_1, P_2, P_3$. Join $P_3$ to $Q$. Through $P_1$ and $P_2$, draw lines parallel to $P_3 Q$ (use the parallel-line construction). These cut $[PQ]$ at the two trisection points.

(b)(i) Triangle $ABC$ has angles: $\angle A = 28°$, $\angle B = 72°$ → $\angle C = 180° - 28° - 72° = 80°$.
Since $DF \parallel BC$, triangle $ADF$ has the same angles: $28°$ at $A$, $72°$ at $D$ (corresponding with $\angle ABC$), $80°$ at $F$ (corresponding with $\angle ACB$).
So the interior angle of triangle $ADF$ at $D$ is $\angle ADF = 72°$. $x$ is the supplementary angle on the line $AB$ → x = 180° − 72° = 108°.
y = 80° (interior angle of triangle $ADF$ at $F$).
z = 108° (same reasoning at $E$ with $EG \parallel BC$).

(b)(ii) Similar because all three pairs of corresponding angles are equal ($28°, 72°, 80°$). Not congruent because the side lengths differ — $|AD| < |AB|$, so triangle $ADF$ is smaller.

(c) Triangle $AEG$ ~ triangle $ABC$ (both have $DF, EG, BC$ parallel, common angle at $A$).
Ratio $\dfrac{|AE|}{|AB|} = \dfrac{20}{30} = \dfrac{2}{3}$.
So $\dfrac{|GE|}{|CB|} = \dfrac{2}{3}$ → $|GE| = \dfrac{2}{3}(14.3) = \dfrac{28.6}{3}$. |GE| ≈ 9.53

Exam Style · HL8 min Word problem — rectangular dimensions from area

A rectangular training grid has its length 3 m more than its width. Its area is 40 m².

(a) Let the width be $x$ m. Write the area as a quadratic equation in $x$.

(b) Solve to find the dimensions of the grid.

Worked solution

(a) Length $= x + 3$. Area $= x(x+3) = 40$ → $x^2 + 3x - 40 = 0$.

(b) Factorise: need product $-40$, sum $3$ → $8$ and $-5$.
$(x + 8)(x - 5) = 0$ → $x = -8$ (reject — can't have negative width) or $x = 5$.
